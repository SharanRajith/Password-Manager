📘 Beginner's Concept: Combinatorics (Counting)

How many tries does it take a hacker to guess your password? Combinatorics is the math of counting possibilities. If you only use lowercase letters, there are 26 options per character. But if you add uppercase, numbers, and symbols, the options explode! "Entropy" is just a math score showing how unimaginably huge those possibilities are.

📘 Beginner's Concept: Clock Math (Modulo)

To encrypt your password, we use "Clock Math". Imagine a clock with 12 hours. If it's 10 o'clock and you add 4 hours, you get 2 o'clock, not 14. We do the same thing, but instead of 12, the clock has trillions of numbers. We spin your password around this giant math clock until it looks completely scrambled.

📘 Beginner's Concept: Unwinding the Clock

When you want to see your password, we use a special "magic number" (your private key). This number perfectly unwinds the giant math clock backwards, landing exactly on your original password. Without the magic number, a hacker has no idea how far back to spin it!
